5 Tips for a Perfect Steak

Choose the Right Cut: The quality of the steak starts with the cut. For a juicy and tender steak, opt for cuts like ribeye, filet mignon, or New York strip. These cuts have good marbling, which helps them stay tender and flavorful during cooking. Bring the Steak to Room Temperature: Before cooking, allow the steak to sit at room temperature for about 30-45 minutes. This ensures even cooking, as a cold steak straight from the fridge will cook unevenly, with the outside overcooked and the inside undercooked. Season Generously: Don’t be shy with the seasoning! Coat the steak with a generous amount of salt and freshly ground black pepper right before cooking. You can also add garlic powder, onion powder, or your favorite seasoning blend for extra flavor. Preheat the Pan or Grill: Whether you're cooking on a grill or in a skillet, make sure it's properly preheated. A hot pan or grill ensures a great sear, which locks in juices and flavors. A sizzling sound when the steak hits the surface is a good sign. Let It Rest After Cooking: After cooking, let the steak rest for at least 5-10 minutes before cutting. This allows the juices to redistribute, keeping the steak juicy and tender. If you cut into it immediately, the juices will run out, making the steak dry. Following these simple tips will help you achieve a perfect, mouth-watering steak every time!
